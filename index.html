02 · Protocol Deployment

Where PQC is live in real protocols

Production

TLS 1.3 Hybrid

X25519+ML-KEM-768 default in Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Brave, Opera. 50% of Cloudflare traffic. ~2 KB overhead, no latency regression.

Standards Track

IPsec / IKEv2

RFC 9370 multi-KEM. ML-KEM drafts for hybrid and standalone. RFC 8784 PSK interim protection.

Standards Track

X.509 Certificates

LAMPS WG: ML-DSA cert IDs, composite certs. 15 KB+ chain sizes remain the bottleneck.

Deployed

iMessage & Signal

Apple PQ3 and Signal PQXDH integrate ML-KEM for quantum-safe E2EE on billions of devices.

Deployed

AWS KMS & OpenSSL 3.5

Built-in ML-KEM, ML-DSA, SLH-DSA support. AWS Private CA ML-DSA certs in GovCloud.

Framework

HPKE (RFC 9180)

KEM-agnostic hybrid encryption. Active draft defines PQ algorithms for modular swap-in.

04 · QKD vs. PQC

The quantum key distribution debate

Industry consensus: PQC is the primary path. QKD is complementary for specific high-security niches.

PQC

Primary path
  • ✓ Software-based — no special hardware
  • ✓ Any distance, existing networks
  • ✓ NIST standardized
  • ✓ Deployed at 50%+ of TLS traffic
  • ✓ Provides authentication
vs

QKD

Complementary
  • △ Needs dedicated fiber/satellite hardware
  • △ Distance limited, trusted nodes needed
  • △ Physics-based (information-theoretic)
  • △ Cannot authenticate on its own
  • △ Expensive, point-to-point only

Who opposes QKD?

  • NSA — "does not support usage of QKD" for NSS
  • ANSSI + BSI + AIVD + Sweden — joint paper on QKD limitations (Jan 2024)
  • GSMA — scopes QKD out of PQC guidelines
  • ASD (Australia) — does not endorse QKD
  • US DoD — QKD prohibited without exception approval

Who deploys QKD?

  • China — strongest proponent. China Telecom: first distributed QKD+PQC (May 2025)
  • EuroQCI — pan-EU infra. Eagle-1 satellite late 2026
  • Singapore — NQSN+ hybrid QKD-PQC network
  • SK Telecom — 330 km backbone + PQC hybrid
  • BT/Toshiba — UK's first commercial QKD metro network
  • Deutsche Telekom — ~2,000 km fiber test network

Satellite PQC

  • QuSecure — first E2E PQC satellite link via SpaceX Starlink (Mar 2023)
  • WISeKey/SEALSQ — WISeSat 4.0 LEO sats with PQC chips (Mar 2026), 100-sat constellation
  • ESA Eagle-1 — QKD satellite for EuroQCI (late 2026)
  • Challenges: 10–20 yr lifetimes, limited OTA updates, constrained processing
05 · Sector Deep Dives

Industry-specific challenges and progress

Scale

US federal gov must migrate NSS, civilian, weapons, IoT, OT, unmanned platforms, classified systems. DoD CIO memo (Nov 2025): inventory all crypto, designate PQC leads in 20 days.

$7.1B estimated cost (2025–2035) with "high uncertainty."

FIPS 140-3 bottleneck

500+ day average validation (42% increase). PQC-validated modules not broadly available before 2027. All FIPS 140-2 certs Historical by Sep 2026.

  • NSS: CNSA 2.0, NIAP validation, pure PQC, Level 5 only
  • FCEB: NIST standards, hybrid OK, OMB M-23-02

Legacy constraints

SCADA/ICS, weapons systems, field sensors with hardcoded crypto. Some systems may never migrate — lacking processing speed, memory, or bandwidth for PQC.

BIS Project Leap

Phase 1 (2023): Hybrid encryption Paris↔Frankfurt.

Phase 2 (Dec 2025): PQC in TARGET2 RTGS with SWIFT, Bank of Italy, Nexi-Colt. All scenarios succeeded.

"Not a simple algorithm swap" — BIS

Card payments — "telescoping"

CBDCs

09 · Conclusions

Three findings the literature under-emphasizes

FINDING 01

Solved problem masks unsolved one

Key exchange works (ML-KEM, 50% traffic). Authentication blocked: 15 KB cert chains, 60%+ slowdowns, no production fix. Half quantum-safe.

FINDING 02

EU hybrid mandate = global standard

EU requires hybrid for lattice PQC. Global banks/telcos serve Europe → hybrid is the binding constraint. Conflicts with US pure-PQC mandate.

FINDING 03

The gap is temporal, not technical

Migration: 12–15+ years. Threat: 5–10 years. The 5–10 year vulnerability window is mathematical. HNDL means the clock started years ago.
